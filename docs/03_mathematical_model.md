# 03 — The Mathematical Model, in Full

*This document develops the optimization architecture proposed in the project's source sketch into the exact program the code solves. It is written for the intelligent non-specialist: every equation is followed by its plain-language meaning, and every parameter by its evidentiary status. The reader who works through it owns the model.*

---

## 0. Relationship to the original sketch

The source document proposed: a welfare objective $W(x,t)=\alpha_1 U_{\text{Growth}}+\alpha_2 U_{\text{Health}}+\alpha_3 U_{\text{Equity}}-\alpha_4\mathbb{D}(t)$; an intertemporal budget constraint $\sum_i x_i + rD_{\text{past}} \le R(t,Y)+\Delta D$; a Laffer boundary $\partial R/\partial t_j \ge \epsilon$; and symbiosis floors $x_k \ge \beta_k Y$. All four survive. The refinements below are: **(a)** the Laffer boundary is no longer imposed as a side constraint but *emerges endogenously* from behavioral erosion terms inside $R(t,Y)$ — which is methodologically stronger, because the revenue function then tells you *where* the peak is rather than assuming you know it; **(b)** a security/defense utility component and a debt-trajectory penalty are added, without which the conservative and fiscal-hawk scenarios cannot even be expressed; **(c)** the single-period budget constraint is replaced by an eleven-year debt recursion, because a one-year constraint cannot see the interest-compounding problem that dominates the CBO outlook; **(d)** political and administrative feasibility enter as explicit box and capacity constraints, since a budget that cannot be enacted optimizes nothing.

## 1. Decision variables

$$z = (\delta_1,\dots,\delta_{18},\ \tau_{\text{ind}},\tau_{\text{corp}},\tau_{\text{cap}},\tau_{\text{pcap}},\tau_{\text{vat}},\tau_{\text{co2}}) \in \mathbb{R}^{24}$$

Each $\delta_i$ is the *fractional change* to spending category $i$ from its FY2026 baseline $x_i^0$ (so $x_i = x_i^0(1+\delta_i)$); the eighteen categories and baselines are in Document 02 §3. The six tax instruments are: change in the average effective individual rate (points), change in the statutory corporate rate (points), adoption fraction of a capital-income reform package, adoption fraction of lifting the payroll taxable maximum, a value-added tax rate (percent), and a carbon price ($/tCO₂e). *Plain language: the model's "steering wheel" has twenty-four axes — eighteen spending dials and six revenue dials.*

## 2. Revenue functions with endogenous behavior

Static revenue scoring (rate × base) systematically overstates yield because bases shrink when rates rise. Each instrument therefore carries its empirically estimated erosion:

**Individual.** $\Delta R_{\text{ind}} = \kappa_{\text{ind}}\,\tau_{\text{ind}}\big(1 - \eta_{\text{ETI}}\,|\tau_{\text{ind}}|/10\big)$, with $\kappa_{\text{ind}} \approx \$300\text{B/point}$ (MEDIUM) and the elasticity of taxable income $\eta_{\text{ETI}}=0.25$, the central value of the Saez–Slemrod–Giertz survey range 0.12–0.40 (LOW).

**Corporate.** $\Delta R_{\text{corp}} = \kappa_c\tau_{\text{corp}} - B_c\,\sigma\,\tau_{\text{corp}}(1+0.04\,\tau_{\text{corp}}^+)$, where $B_c=\$480$B is the corporate base, and $\sigma=0.012$ is the profit-shifting/capital-flight semi-elasticity (meta-analytic range ≈0.8–1.5%/point; LOW). The convex second term *is* the Laffer mechanism: marginal revenue declines as the rate climbs, and the model can locate the peak rather than being told it. This term is the formal encoding of the entanglement Channel discussed in Document 01 §3 — mobile capital disciplines the tax instrument inside the mathematics.

**Capital-income package and payroll-cap lift** are scored as adoption fractions times post-behavioral maximum yields ($120B and $180B/yr; JCT-style and SSA-actuary ranges; LOW and MEDIUM).

**VAT.** $\Delta R_{\text{vat}} = B_v\frac{\tau_{\text{vat}}}{100}(1-e_v)$ with base $B_v\approx\$12.7$T (~40% of GDP after exemptions) and erosion $e_v=0.15$.

**Carbon.** $\Delta R_{\text{co2}} = Q\,\tau_{\text{co2}}(1-d\,\tau_{\text{co2}})$, $Q=4.0$ GtCO₂e covered, $d=0.006$: revenue *self-erodes as it succeeds*, because the point of the tax is to shrink its own base. The erosion is simultaneously credited as avoided damages (§4).

## 3. The endogenous economy

GDP responds to the budget. First-year demand effect:
$$Y = Y_0 + \sum_i m_i\,\Delta x_i + \sum_j m^{\tau}_j\,\Delta R_j,$$
with spending multipliers $m_i$ from CBO's own scoring ranges and the Ramey (2019) survey — transfers to liquidity-constrained households ≈1.2, infrastructure ≈0.9, defense ≈0.7, etc. — and tax multipliers $m^\tau_j<0$ (a VAT drags demand at ≈−0.9 per dollar; capital taxes ≈−0.2 to −0.35). All LOW–MEDIUM confidence; identified as a primary sensitivity axis. Revenue then feeds back: $\Delta R_{\text{feedback}} = 0.175\,(Y-Y_0)$, the average revenue share of GDP. Separately, sustained public investment shifts *potential growth*: $g = g_0 + \sum_i \gamma_i\,\Delta x_i$, with supply-side coefficients $\gamma_i$ nonzero only for R&D, education, transportation, public health, and environment, calibrated conservatively from the public-capital and R&D-returns literatures (LOW). *Plain language: a dollar moved is not a dollar in a vacuum — it changes the size of the economy it is drawn from, this year through demand and over the decade through capacity.*

## 4. The objective: a social welfare function with stated values

$$\max_z\ W(z) = \alpha_g U_{\text{growth}} + \alpha_h U_{\text{health}} + \alpha_e U_{\text{equity}} + \alpha_s U_{\text{security}} - \alpha_d \mathbb{D} - \alpha_b \mathbb{B}$$

All components share one unit — **1.0 ≡ $100B of social value** — so the α-weights are pure value statements, not unit conversions. The program-utility components use a logarithmic form, $U = \sum_i \theta_i \frac{x_i^0}{100}\ln(1+\delta_i)$: concave (the first added dollar buys more well-being than the millionth — diminishing returns) and asymmetric (cuts are penalized convexly — taking the last dollar from a thin program hurts more than the first dollar helped). The category weights $\theta_i$ are calibrated to the **Marginal Value of Public Funds** literature (Hendren & Sprung-Keyser, *QJE* 2020): spending whose measured MVPF exceeds 1 — children's health coverage, public-health infrastructure, education — carries $\theta>1$. **These θ are the most ethically loaded numbers in the model (LOW confidence by construction), which is precisely why they are isolated in one place in `data.py` and why Document 04's sensitivity section attacks them first.** $U_{\text{growth}}$ is the demand-level effect plus the supply-side increment capitalized over the decade at a conservative factor of 25. $U_{\text{equity}}$ adds tax-side incidence (capital-income and payroll-cap instruments score progressive; a VAT scores regressive at −0.8 index points per $100B). $\mathbb{D}$ is deadweight loss: marginal excess burden per dollar raised (individual 0.35, corporate 0.30, VAT 0.20, carbon 0.05 — the carbon tax is nearly free *as a tax* because it corrects a mispricing) plus a quadratic curvature term in normalized instrument intensity. $\mathbb{B}$ is a softplus penalty on the terminal debt ratio exceeding its 2026 level — smooth, so the optimizer feels the cliff before falling off it.

## 5. Constraints — where reality pushes back

**(C1) Debt dynamics** (replaces the one-period budget constraint): $D_{t+1} = D_t + P_t + r_t D_t$, with primary deficit $P_t$ scaling with nominal growth, and the effective interest rate $r_t$ relaxing from 3.45% toward the ~4.3% marginal rate as the stock rolls over (12%/yr). **Validation: with all dials at zero this recursion reproduces CBO's published path — 101% of GDP (2026) → 121% (2036) versus CBO's 120% — to within one point, using no fitted parameters.** Scenario-specific caps then bind it: a year-one deficit ceiling and/or a 2036 debt-ratio ceiling. **(C2) Statutory floors/ceilings**: each $\delta_i$ is boxed by what one budget cycle can lawfully and operationally move — mandatory programs ±3–10% (entitlement law changes slowly), discretionary ±15–60%. **(C3) Symbiosis floors** (Document 01 made flesh): defense ≥ 2.5% of endogenous GDP; Medicare+Medicaid jointly ≥ 90% of baseline in any one cycle, because their private administrative layer cannot absorb faster contraction without delivery failure. **(C4) Reallocation capacity**: $\sum_i |x_i^0\delta_i| \le 12\%$ of programmatic spending — agencies, statutes, and appropriations committees have finite throughput (implemented with a smooth $\sqrt{u^2+\varepsilon}$ absolute value so the solver's gradients exist). **(C5) Instrument bounds**, including per-scenario political exclusions (a coalition that will not enact a VAT gets $\tau_{\text{vat}}\equiv 0$, and so on).

## 6. Solution method and its honest limits

The program is a smooth nonlinear constrained problem in 24 variables, solved by **SLSQP** (sequential least-squares quadratic programming — the method the source sketch anticipated), with multi-start initialization (baseline plus four perturbed starts) to guard against local optima, and acceptance of solutions pinned to active constraint surfaces when the maximum constraint violation is numerically negligible. Runtime: seconds. Limits, stated plainly: the macro block is a *scalarized reduced form*, not a general-equilibrium model — it will not forecast recessions, and its multipliers are contested ranges, not constants; behavioral parameters (ETI, profit-shifting, VAT erosion) carry wide bands; the θ-weights import the MVPF literature's own identification debates; lobbying is constrained, not simulated; and the eleven-year recursion assumes no crises. The companion discipline is therefore Document 04's sensitivity and tripwire sections: the model's job is not to be right, it is to be *checkably wrong in stated ways* — which is what separates an instrument from an oracle.
